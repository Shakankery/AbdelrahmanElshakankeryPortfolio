Know Your Machine!

The Laser cutter we used in fablab is LASER EG-1080. It’s a 80/150 WATT with bed dimensions of 80x1000.

It is very important before operating on any machine to check the safety instructions. in our case the instructions is on top of the machine, but here are some general rule to operate it

  • Never leave the machine unattended.
  • Only cut materials that you can cut with the laser cutter . if you are not sure ask someone.
  • Never forget to turn on the air compressor / filter , otherwise you might destroy the laser. Moreover after finishing a job wait for a bit before you open the lid.
  • Make sure you know what to do in case of fire .

The machine Used With different Non-Metal raw materials like:

( Wood – Acrylic – Leather – Cloths – Paper )

To determine the optimal power and speed we created two tests one to cut the wood the other on to scan it using different speeds and powers.

  • First trial in which the speed was to fast that it only cuts ont 80p/50s

2- Kerf test

The laser burns away a portion of material when it cuts through. This is known as the laser kerf and ranges from 0.08mm – 1mm depending on the material type and other conditional factors. for more info here.

  • For power 60 and speed 45 the kerf=0.1mm
3- Picture Test

We change the power and speed of our laser according to our engraving test to change the color.

Step 2: The concept behind parametric design

For someone that come from the electronics and programming world I wasn't famelier with the concept of parametric design. before that my steps in mechanicle design was so simple "Draw, Dimension, Extrude" this could work just fine until I need to edit my design !!

So the simple way to understand parametric desgin is to think of as design based on parameters. you could think of Parameters as variables so insteade of putting number in your dimensions you end up putting the parameters you want.

So now parameters gives us the flexiability to edit our design but in a constrain way that we always end up with something that works.
